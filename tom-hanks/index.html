Tom Hanks Forrest Gump

“Forrest Gump” is a 1994 American comedy-drama film directed by Robert Zemeckis and starring Tom Hanks in the titular role.
The movie is based on the 1986 novel of the same name by Winston Groom. Hanks won the Academy Award for Best Actor for his portrayal of Forrest Gump, a slow-witted but kind-hearted and athletically-talented man from Alabama who witnesses and unwittingly influences several defining historical events in the 20th century, including the Vietnam War and the Watergate scandal.
The film was a critical and commercial success, grossing over $677 million worldwide and receiving 13 Oscar nominations, winning six of them, including Best Picture.

Tom Hanks Castaway

“Cast Away” is a 2000 American survival drama film directed and produced by Robert Zemeckis and starring Tom Hanks. In the film, Hanks plays the role of Chuck Noland, a FedEx executive who is stranded on an uninhabited island in the South Pacific after his plane crashes.
The film follows Noland’s physical and emotional struggle to survive alone on the island for four years, before he finally makes his way back to civilization. The film was a critical and commercial success, and Hanks received widespread acclaim for his performance.
